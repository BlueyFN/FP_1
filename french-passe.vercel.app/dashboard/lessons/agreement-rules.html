Master when and how to make past participles agree

Understanding Agreement Rules

In French, past participles sometimes need to agree in gender and number with other parts of the sentence. These agreement rules are essential for speaking and writing correctly.

Key Points

  • Agreement depends on which auxiliary verb is used (avoir or être)
  • With être, the past participle always agrees with the subject
  • With avoir, agreement only happens in specific situations
  • Agreement means adding -e for feminine and -s for plural

Basic Agreement Patterns

Masculine singular: no change

Feminine singular: add -e

Masculine plural: add -s

Feminine plural: add -es

  • Il est allé. (He went.)
  • Elle est allée. (She went.)
  • Ils sont allés. (They went.)
  • Elles sont allées. (They went.)
